Field Trippers is a field trip service that gives children opportunities for experiential learning with their peers.

Each of our expeditions is specially designed to push our trippers out of their comfort zone, get them connecting with their community, and developing attributes like courage, empathy, humility, and perseverance.

We get down and dirty; embrace mess, mistakes, and mishaps; and laugh till our cheeks hurt.

Each month of the semester follows a different theme:

  • Wilderness Adventure
  • How It’s Made
  • Community Service
  • The Arts
  • People and Cultures

Here’s an example semester itinerary:

August: Explore the Spanish Fork Dripping Rock trail and learn all about moss and lychee, how it contributes to our ecosystem, and what we can do to help protect it.

September: Visit a working farm to learn how some of our food and fibers are harvested: milk goats, sheer sheep, spin honey, and harvest fruit.

October: Volunteer at the Food Bank, learning about food scarcity, helping stock shelves and giving love notes to the visitors.

November: Attend an orchestra performance followed by a special lesson with a professional percussionist to learn all the different instruments they play!

December: Visit a Native American family who teaches us the bitter sweet history of fry bread, makes some with us, and shares a bit of their people’s music and dance.
